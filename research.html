Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence, human computer interaction, and embedded systems, materializing in the form of wearable and ubiquitous computers. I work in the Ubicomp Group and the Contextual Computing Group at Georgia Tech on mobile health applications and novel user interfaces.

Wearable Sensors for Eating Detection

Food journaling is often prescribed by physicians to help combat chronic diseases such as diabetes by stimulating self-reflection about one's eating habits. This project demonstrates a wearable device to help users not forget to journal their meals.   Read more >>>

Low Cost Virtual Reality Sensing and Interactions

Virtual reality headsets have become increasingly accessible to consumers due to their declining costs. This series of works makes use of sensing techniques for enhancing the cheap and mobile virtual reality experience.   Coming soon!

Audio Life Logging

Information is at an unprecedented level of convenience thanks to mobile and wearable devices. However, these small devices are not nearly as convienent for input. We're exploring cues in everyday speech that can be leveraged for automatic logging.   Coming soon!

Enhancing Traditional Piano Lessons

Piano lessons traditionally consist of students watching and listening to the teacher demonstrate a passage of music, and then attempting to mimic it. Tactile Teacher allows teachers to also share the tactile sensation of tapping the piano keys to help students better prepare for their own attempt.   Read more >>>
